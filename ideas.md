# Visio — Indigenous Community Media Lab: Design Ideas

## Design Approach Options

<idea>
**Design Movement**: Raw Editorial Modernism meets Indigenous Land Aesthetics
**Core Principles**:
1. Earth-rooted warmth — terracotta, deep ochre, warm cream, and forest green as the primary palette, evoking land, fire, and growth
2. Editorial boldness — oversized serif headlines that feel like documentary film titles, not tech startups
3. Asymmetric tension — layouts that break the grid intentionally, mirroring the non-linear nature of oral storytelling
4. Texture as meaning — grain, film-grain overlays, and rough-edged dividers that feel handmade and human

**Color Philosophy**:
- Deep Terracotta (#C1440E / oklch equivalent) — primary action color, warmth, fire, resilience
- Warm Cream (#F5EDD8) — background, breath, space
- Forest Green (#2D5016) — secondary, growth, land, nature
- Charcoal (#1C1C1C) — text, depth, gravity
- Warm Amber (#D4820A) — accent, highlight, energy

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Asymmetric split layouts with large image panels offset against text blocks
- Film-strip inspired horizontal scroll sections for the story archive
- Overlapping elements that create depth (text over images with semi-transparent blocks)
- Full-bleed hero with diagonal crop into content

**Signature Elements**:
1. Film frame borders — thin double-line borders that reference 16mm film frames
2. Circular "lens" motifs — subtle circular elements that echo camera lenses and Indigenous circular symbolism
3. Rough-edge section dividers — SVG wave or torn-paper dividers between sections

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Slow, deliberate hover states — nothing snappy or tech-y; transitions feel considered
- Parallax scrolling on hero images to create cinematic depth
- Video thumbnails that expand on hover with a subtle film-grain overlay

**Animation**:
- Entrance animations: text slides up from below, images fade in with slight scale
- Hover: cards lift with warm shadow, images subtly zoom
- Section transitions: fade with slight vertical drift
- No bouncy or playful animations — everything is grounded and intentional

**Typography System**:
- Display: Playfair Display (bold, italic for emphasis) — editorial, literary, timeless
- Body: Source Serif 4 — warm, readable, humanist
- UI/Labels: DM Sans — clean, modern contrast to the serif heaviness
- Hierarchy: Large display (80-120px) → Section headers (40-56px) → Body (18px) → Labels (13px uppercase tracked)
</idea>

## CHOSEN APPROACH: Raw Editorial Modernism meets Indigenous Land Aesthetics

This approach best captures the dual identity of Visio: a serious, credible media organization with deep community roots. The editorial boldness honors the documentary tradition (POV, Sundance), while the earth palette and asymmetric layouts feel Indigenous and grounded rather than corporate. The film-frame motifs connect the visual language directly to the medium Visio works in.
